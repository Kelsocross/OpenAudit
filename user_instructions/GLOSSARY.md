# Glossary of Terms

Common freight and shipping terms used in OpenAudit Platform.

---

## General Shipping Terms

**Carrier**
The shipping company that transports packages (e.g., FedEx, UPS, USPS, DHL).

**Tracking Number**
A unique identifier assigned to each shipment for tracking and reference purposes.

**Service Type**
The level of shipping service selected (e.g., Ground, Express, Overnight, 2-Day).

**Zone**
Geographic pricing area used by carriers to calculate shipping costs. Higher zones typically mean greater distances and higher costs.

**Billed Amount**
The total amount charged by the carrier for a shipment, including base rate and any surcharges.

**Actual Weight**
The physical weight of a package as measured on a scale.

**Dimensional (DIM) Weight**
A calculated weight based on package dimensions (length x width x height / divisor). Carriers charge based on whichever is greater: actual weight or DIM weight.

---

## Billing Error Types

**Late Delivery**
When a carrier fails to deliver a package by the guaranteed delivery date/time. Often eligible for refund under carrier's money-back guarantee.

**DIM Weight Overcharge**
When the carrier incorrectly calculates the dimensional weight, resulting in overcharges.

**Duplicate Tracking**
When a single shipment is billed multiple times under different tracking numbers or on different invoices.

**Incorrect Zone**
When the carrier bills for the wrong shipping zone, typically charging a higher zone than actual.

**Address Type Mismatch**
When the carrier incorrectly classifies the delivery address (e.g., billing residential surcharge for a commercial address).

**Unnecessary Surcharge**
Additional fees that were charged but should not have been applied based on service agreement or shipment characteristics.

**Disputable Surcharge**
Surcharges that may not be justified and can be challenged for refund.

**Service Type Mismatch**
When the billed service level doesn't match the service actually used or requested.

---

## Refund and Claims Terms

**Claim**
A formal request submitted to a carrier for a refund due to billing errors or service failures.

**Refund Estimate**
The projected amount that can be recovered from a billing error.

**Dispute Reason**
The specific justification for requesting a refund from the carrier.

**Actionable Error**
A billing error that qualifies for a refund claim under carrier policies.

**Claim Type**
The category of claim being submitted (e.g., Late Delivery, Overcharge, Lost Package).

**Bulk Upload**
Submitting multiple claims at once through a carrier's online portal, typically using a formatted spreadsheet.

**Claims Report**
A detailed document listing all claims being submitted, including tracking numbers, amounts, and justifications.

---

## Rate and Contract Terms

**Base Rate**
The fundamental shipping charge before any surcharges or discounts are applied.

**Surcharge**
Additional fees added to the base rate for specific services or circumstances (e.g., residential delivery, fuel, peak season).

**Fuel Surcharge**
A variable fee added to shipments to account for fuel price fluctuations.

**Residential Surcharge**
An additional fee charged for deliveries to residential addresses vs. commercial locations.

**Peak Season Surcharge**
Extra charges applied during high-volume shipping periods (typically holidays).

**Carrier Contract**
A formal agreement between a shipper and carrier outlining negotiated rates, discounts, and service terms.

**Benchmark**
Industry-standard rates or performance metrics used for comparison.

**Negotiated Rate**
A customized shipping rate agreed upon between the shipper and carrier, typically lower than published rates.

---

## Audit and Analysis Terms

**Audit**
The process of systematically reviewing shipping invoices to identify billing errors and overcharges.

**Findings**
Billing errors and discrepancies identified during the audit process.

**Potential Savings**
The total amount that can be recovered from identified billing errors.

**Affected Shipments**
The number of shipments that contain billing errors or issues.

**Error Distribution**
The breakdown showing how different types of errors are spread across your shipments.

**Timeline Chart**
A visual representation showing overcharges and error frequency over time.

---

## Platform-Specific Terms

**Upload & Audit**
The section where you upload invoice data and run automated billing error analysis.

**Refund Recovery**
The section where you manage and submit claims for identified billing errors.

**Q&A**
A frequently asked questions page that provides expert answers to common shipping and freight questions.

**Dashboard**
A visual overview showing audit results, savings metrics, and analytical charts.

**Contract Review**
The section for analyzing carrier contracts and receiving negotiation strategies.

**Bulk Upload Format**
A standardized Excel file format required by carriers (like FedEx) for submitting multiple claims at once.

---

## Data and File Terms

**CSV (Comma-Separated Values)**
A plain text file format where data is separated by commas. Commonly used for invoice data.

**Excel/XLSX**
Microsoft Excel spreadsheet format, widely used for shipping invoices and claims files.

**Data Validation**
The process of checking uploaded data for completeness, accuracy, and proper formatting.

**Required Columns**
Essential data fields that must be present in uploaded files (e.g., Tracking Number, Carrier, Date).

---

## Service Level Terms

**Ground Service**
Standard ground transportation, typically the slowest and most economical option.

**Express Service**
Expedited delivery with guaranteed faster transit times.

**Overnight**
Next-business-day delivery service.

**2-Day Service**
Delivery guaranteed within two business days.

**International Service**
Shipping to destinations outside the domestic country.

**Domestic Service**
Shipping within the same country.

---

## Performance Metrics

**Success Rate**
The percentage of shipments delivered without billing errors or service failures.

**Total Shipments**
The complete count of packages analyzed during an audit period.

**Shipments with Issues**
The number of shipments that contain billing errors or service problems.

**Recovery Rate**
The percentage of claimed refunds that are successfully recovered from carriers.

**ROI (Return on Investment)**
The value gained from recovered refunds compared to the cost of the auditing service.

---

## Carrier-Specific Terms

**FedEx**
A major carrier offering various domestic and international shipping services.

**UPS**
United Parcel Service, a major shipping carrier with global reach.

**USPS**
United States Postal Service, the government-run mail and package delivery service.

**DHL**
International shipping and logistics company specializing in express delivery.

**Money-Back Guarantee**
A carrier policy that promises refunds for late deliveries or service failures.

---

## Technical Terms

**Browser**
The software application used to access the web platform (e.g., Chrome, Firefox, Edge).

**Upload**
The process of transferring files from your computer to the platform.

**Download**
The process of saving files from the platform to your computer.

**Filter**
A tool to narrow down displayed data based on specific criteria (e.g., date range, carrier, error type).

**Export**
The process of saving audit results or data to a file format like Excel or PDF.

**Session**
Your active period of using the platform from login to logout.

## Best Practice Terms

**Data Hygiene**
Maintaining clean, accurate, and well-organized shipping data.

**Regular Audits**
Conducting invoice reviews on a consistent schedule (weekly, monthly, etc.).

**Claim Documentation**
Keeping organized records of all submitted claims and their outcomes.

**Benchmark Comparison**
Evaluating your rates and performance against industry standards.

**Rate Optimization**
The process of improving shipping costs through better rates, carrier selection, and service choices.
